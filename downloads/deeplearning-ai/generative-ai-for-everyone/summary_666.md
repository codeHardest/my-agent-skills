## 详细内容（保留所有原始信息）

### 一、AGI概念的澄清

视频开头指出，AGI（人工通用智能）是一个令人兴奋的概念，但围绕着它存在一些混淆。这种混淆很大程度上源于"general"这个词的使用方式。正如大家所知，AI是一项通用目的技术（general purpose technology），意味着它对许多不同的事物都有用。然而，一个通用目的技术并不等同于人工通用智能（AGI）。这是两个完全不同的概念，需要明确区分。

### 二、AGI的精确定义

视频深入探讨了AGI的技术定义。最广泛接受的AGI定义是：AI能够完成任何人类可以完成的智力任务。有些定义甚至说任何人类或哺乳动物可以完成的任务，但视频采用了前一个定义。

### 三、AGI的具体能力示例

为了更具体地说明AGI的含义，视频引用了深度学习先驱Yann LeCun提出的例子：

**示例一：驾驶汽车**
- 如果我们有AGI，AI应该能够通过大约20小时的练习学会驾驶汽车
- 这类似于一个青少年能够做到的程度
- 然而，今天的自动驾驶汽车还远未达到这个水平
- 显然不能仅通过如此少的练习时间就学会驾驶

**示例二：完成PhD研究**
- 如果我们有AGI，AI应该能够完成相当于PhD论文水平的研究工作
- 完成这样的研究需要大约五年的工作时间
- 或者可能更快
- 今天，AI可以帮助完成某些头脑风暴和写作的部分
- 也许可以作为某些研究要素的思想伙伴
- 但显然离真正的PhD研究还有很远的距离

**示例三：知识工作者任务**
- 如果我们有AGI，AI应该能够完成程序员或几乎任何知识工作者的几乎所有任务
- 这些工作者的工作贡献就是执行智力任务
- 显然，我们离这个目标还有很长的路要走

### 四、时间预测与观点分歧

视频承认，对于AGI的实现时间存在不同观点。作者本人的观点是：我们距离AGI还有许多十年，甚至可能更长时间。但他希望有生之年能够实现这一目标。

然而，有些企业对AGI的实现时间做出了更为乐观的预测。作者发现，这些企业中的大多数实际上改变了AGI的定义，将门槛降低了很多。当作者将其中一家企业的AGI定义展示给一位经济学家朋友时，这位经济学家的反应是：如果那是AGI的定义，他认为30年前就实现了。

确实，通过充分降低标准，我们可以更快地"实现"AGI。但对于最广泛接受的AGI定义来说，作者认为我们仍然有很长的路要走。

### 五、大语言模型与AGI的展望

视频指出，大语言模型令人兴奋的方面之一是，我们可以将它们用作推理引擎。正如上周提到的，也许我们现在开始看到AGI未来可能是什么样子的大致轮廓。

从物理定律的角度来看，作者不认为有什么基本定律阻止我们创造AGI。他认为AGI实际上将对人类社会非常有价值。但要实现这一目标，我们仍然需要一些重大的技术突破。

### 六、AI与生物智能的对比

视频特别指出，实现AGI的困难之一在于，它将人工智能与人类智能进行基准比较。人工智能和生物智能沿着两条完全不同的路径发展。例如：

- AI可以从远多于任何人类一生能阅读的文本中学习
- AI在某些特定任务上已经远优于任何人类

但是，要求AI做人类可以完成的所有智力任务——所有这些任务——仍然是一个很高的门槛。

### 七、负责任的AI使用

最后，视频强调，尽管我们距离AGI还有一段距离，但AI已经非常强大，我们必须负责任地使用它。下一视频将探讨"负责任的AI"（Responsible AI）这一主题。

AGI, Artificial
General Intelligence is an exciting concept. I think some of the
confusion around it stems from the use
of the word general. As you know, AI is a
general purpose technology, meaning that it is useful
for many different things. The rise of large
language models has led to single models like ChatGPT that are useful for many things and feel like they
could be general purpose. But a general purpose
technology is not the same thing as artificial
general intelligence. Let's take a look
at what really is the technical definition of AGI. The most widely accepted
definition of AGI is AI. They could do any intellectual
task that a human can. Some definitions actually say any tasks that a
human or mammal can, but I'll stick with
this one for now. For example, if we have AGI, AI would be able
to learn to drive a car through about
20 hours of practice, similar to what a teenager can. This is an example raised by Deep Learning Pioneer Yana Khan. Today, self driving cars
still aren't quite there yet. Certainly not after so low
as 20 hours of practice. If we had AGI, AI would
also be able to do the intellectual
task of completing a PhD thesis level research
after five years of work, or maybe even faster. Today, AI can help with some parts of
brainstorming and writing and maybe be a thought partner for some elements of research. But we're clearly very
far away from this still. If we had AGI, AI would also be able to do
pretty much all the tasks of a computer programmer or really any other
knowledge workers whose contributions to work are to carry out intellectual tasks. Clearly, we're still
very far away from this. I know that there are
different views about how long it would
take to get to AGI. I think we're still
many decades away, maybe even longer,
but I hope we'll get there sometime
in a lifetimes. There are some businesses
that have made much more optimistic forecast
of when we'll get there. But I found that most of
those businesses have changed the definition of AGI and set a much lower bar to get there. I showed the definition of AGI used by one of these
businesses to an economist, friend of mine and
he equipped, boy, if that's the definition of AGI, I think we got
there 30 years ago. It's true that by lowering
the bar sufficiently, we could get there much faster. But for the most widely
accepted definition of AGI, I think we're still
quite a long ways away. One of the exciting things about large language models is that we can use them as
reasoning engines, as I mentioned last week. Maybe we're now
starting to see what a rough outline of what
AGI could be some day. I don't think there's any
fundamental laws of physics that prevents us from
ever creating AGI, which I think will actually be very valuable
to human society. But we'll still need
some significant technical breakthroughs
to get there. One of the hard things about
getting to AGI is that it benchmarks artificial
intelligence against human intelligence. Artificial intelligence and
biological intelligence have progress along two
very different paths. For example, AI learning from far more texts than any human
can read in a lifetime. AI is already far better than any human at certain
tasks already. But to ask AI to do everything, all intellectual tasks
that humans can do, that's still just
a very high bar. But even though we're
still away from AGI, AI is very powerful and it's important that we
use it responsibly. Let's take a look in the next
video at Responsible AI.
